Common questions

Japa planning, answered plainly

How much does it actually cost to relocate from Nigeria to Canada?

For a single adult on Express Entry, government fees alone run about CAD 2,300–3,000 (roughly ₦2.3M–₦3M) once you include the processing fee, Right of Permanent Residence Fee, biometrics, a language test and an Educational Credential Assessment. That's before flights, a settling-in buffer, or the separate proof-of-funds amount you must show but don't pay. Run your own numbers on the Japa Relocation Cost Calculator above.

What is Proof of Funds and how much do I need?

Most immigration routes require you to show a minimum bank balance for a set period before you apply — it's money you must have access to, not a fee you pay. For a single applicant on Canada's Express Entry, the 2026 threshold is CAD 15,263 (about ₦15.3M), and it rises with family size. Check your exact threshold and get a savings-gap plan on the Proof of Funds Calculator.

How much does it cost to live in Toronto or London as a Nigerian family?

It varies enormously by city, not just country — a one-bedroom rents for around CAD 2,200 in Toronto versus about CAD 1,700 in Calgary, and around £1,900 in London versus roughly £1,000 in Manchester. Add groceries, transport and bills, and a single newcomer's steady-state monthly cost typically lands somewhere between ₦2.5M and ₦5.5M depending on the city and lifestyle. Run your household size through the Cost of Living Calculator for a specific number.

Are these figures official?

Figures are drawn from official sources (IRCC, UK Home Office) and dated at the time of writing, but immigration fees and thresholds change without much notice. Always confirm the current figure on the relevant government site before you pay anything — these tools are for planning, not a guarantee.
