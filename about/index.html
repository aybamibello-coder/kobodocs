Built in Lagos, for how Nigerians actually get paid

KoboDocs started with a simple frustration: every "free invoice generator" online was built for someone else's market — wrong currency, no VAT, no WHT, no Ajo. We built the one we wished existed.

What we're building

KoboDocs is a suite of free, Naira-native business tools — invoices, receipts, quotations, payslips, budgets, loan schedules, HR letters, waybills, and QR codes — plus an Ajo & Esusu tracker for thrift circles, a set of relocation calculators for anyone planning a move abroad, and standalone calculators for take-home pay, CAC registration costs, and import duty. Every free tool works without an account, because most of the people who need it are running a shop or a side hustle, not filling out onboarding forms.

For businesses that outgrow the free tools, KoboDocs Business Suite adds quotes with one-click convert-to-invoice, a debt dashboard with reminders, a client portal, lightweight bookkeeping, and inventory tracking — everything in one plan, no tier confusion. Recurring invoice automation and in-portal payment collection are on the roadmap.

What we believe

Built for here, not adapted from elsewhere

VAT, WHT, PAYE bands, pension and NHF rates — all reflect current Nigerian rules, not a US template with the currency symbol swapped.

We never touch your money

KoboDocs is a record-keeping tool. We don't hold Ajo/Esusu contributions, disburse loans, or sit between you and your bank.

Free should mean free

No watermarks, no artificial caps on the core document tools, no account required to create a single invoice.

Paid tiers save time, not unlock basics

Pro, Business Suite, the Cooperative Plan and Japa Pass exist for people doing this daily and want automation — never to gate what should be free.
